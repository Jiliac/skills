# Longevity, Mental Health, and Platform Economics

Strategies for sustainable content creation careers and financial models across platforms.

## Durability Over Kaizen

- "Durability over Kaizen" -- don't try to beat your last video every time. That's unsustainable.
- Amixem saw many creators burn out trying to top themselves: in production scale, humor quality, or viral metrics.
- Mister V cited as a rare exception who maintains escalating quality. Most creators cannot replicate this.
- The 2022 realization (after 10 years): "We can stop telling ourselves this will end next Tuesday." Focus shifted from growth to stability.
- Marathon, not a sprint. Basic phrase, but the core operating principle.
- Provide stability to yourself, your audience, and your employees.

## Mental Health by Platform

### YouTube

- Analytics can be anxiogenic. Joyca documented a burnout caused by stat addiction on his channel.
- YouTube Studio intentionally shows positive comments first -- a design choice to reduce creator anxiety.
- Analytics phrasing is softened ("it will work better next time" instead of harsh failure metrics).
- Still not perfect. When a video underperforms, the instinct is "my career is over" even when rationally you know it's not.
- Bad buzz feels career-ending in the moment but is forgotten within a month by everyone except the creator.
- Bug-caused view drops (-40%) can trigger existential crises before the technical cause is identified.

### Twitch

- Worst platform for mental health among all content platforms.
- Cannot take breaks without algorithmic punishment.
- Cannot pre-produce content (live-only format).
- Constant guilt when not streaming.
- The combination of these three factors creates a mental health trap.

### General Creator Psychology

- When you're fatigued or going through personal issues, even small negative feedback feels catastrophic.
- Conversations with major YouTubers during their "bad buzz" moments: they're convinced it's career-ending, even when outsiders see it as trivial.
- The irrationality is universal and affects even the most experienced creators.

## Financial Stability Strategy

### Revenue Diversification

- YouTube dual revenue: AdSense + sponsor integrations. Both are necessary; neither works alone.
- Secure annual sponsor contracts (4-6 major deals) at the start of each year.
- This provides 4-6 months of payroll coverage regardless of individual video performance.
- Build a "war chest" (tresor de guerre) for downturns. Lesson from Amixem's parents who nearly lost everything in the 2008 financial crisis.

### Covid Lesson

- When Covid hit, half of sponsors pulled out overnight.
- Pre-signed annual contracts saved the business.
- New sponsors (gaming, online services) filled some gaps.
- The experience reinforced the importance of signed contracts over one-shot deals.

### Internalizing vs. Outsourcing

- Amixem internalized everything: 30 CDI employees (full-time contracts), no freelancers for core production.
- Province location (Angers) makes CDI employees viable where Paris would require more expensive intermittents.
- Monthly studio cost in Angers = half-day rental cost in Paris. The gap is enormous.
- Disadvantage: harder to recruit specialists to a non-Paris location. Most team members relocated to Angers.
- Advantage: employees are loyal, deeply integrated, and always available. No scrambling for freelancers.

### The 30-Person Threshold

- Growth difficulties at every multiple of 3: solo -> 3 (disagreements possible) -> 9 (managers needed) -> 30 (managers of managers, political dynamics) -> 100+.
- At 30, you regain focus on your core value (making content) because operational management is delegated.
- Below 10, you recruit to share the load but your days get worse, not better (HR problems, interruptions).

## Province vs. Paris

- Angers works as well as Paris for YouTube. Unlike TV, which requires Paris proximity for guests and production infrastructure.
- Financial advantage: studio construction costs 2-3M in Angers for 850-900m2 of shooting space + 1,500m2 of offices. Impossible in Paris at that price.
- Protection from overexposure: in Angers, Amixem is recognized but has a "cocoon." In Paris, the constant public exposure is draining (Camille Combal comparison).
- Guest logistics are the main disadvantage: flying people to Angers costs time and money. This is improving as the channel grows (Pierre Niney came to Angers).
- Province life is an intentional choice for longevity, not a compromise.

## Sport as Mental Reset

- Amixem (self-described non-sportif) started light jogging.
- Time dilation effect: thought 25 minutes had passed, only 10 minutes had elapsed.
- Realization: "You spend all day complaining about not having enough time. Here, you found how to slow down time."
- In 10-year vision: "master my time and relearn slowness."

## Side Businesses and Diversification

- Spacefox (textile/jewelry brand with meteorite bracelets, Akrone watches): creative outlet, separate business.
- Star Smash (burger brand via Taster dark kitchens): joint-venture model, available in 50+ cities from day one.
- Sputnik (internal marketing/influence agency): handles Amixem's deals + represents other creators.
- Diversification provides financial safety net if content creation stops, without requiring content business to be "sellable."
- The "key person" problem: Amixem without Maxime is worth nothing (like selling "the brand Gad Elmaleh" without Gad). Accept this rather than fighting it.

| ------------- | ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | --------------- | -------------------------- |
