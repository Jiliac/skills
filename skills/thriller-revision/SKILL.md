# Thriller Revision

Revise historical fiction manuscripts to function as political thrillers using Robert Harris's method. This skill executes four diagnostic revision passes to eliminate research-flexing, maintain thriller velocity, and ensure political urgency.

## Mental Mode

"Is this gripping?"

This skill focuses exclusively on making the manuscript work as a THRILLER. Historical authenticity auditing comes later. The question is: Does this create urgency, tension, and forward momentum?

## When to Use

Use this skill after completing your first draft when you need to:

- Make historical politics feel urgent and modern (not academic)
- Cut exposition dumps and embed info in dialogue/action
- Eliminate research-flexing and pacing bloat
- Audit thriller structure across the full manuscript
- Ensure stakes escalate properly

This is revision work for THRILLER elements only. For historical authenticity review (dialogue polish, anachronism sweep), use the `historical-authenticity-review` skill after completing these passes.

## The Four Revision Passes

Execute these passes sequentially. Each pass has a specific diagnostic focus.

### Pass 1: Modern Analog Filter

**Goal:** Make historical politics feel urgent and contemporary (not distant/academic)

**Diagnostic question:** Does this political conflict feel as urgent as a modern boardroom coup or Washington scandal?

**Method:** Translate period-specific political mechanics into modern analogies. If a scene reads like a history lecture, ask: "How would this play out in a modern political thriller?" Adjust tone to match that urgency while keeping period details.

See `references/modern-analog-filter.md` for the full translation table and examples.

### Pass 2: Exposition Extraction

**Goal:** Cut info-dumps and embed historical context in dialogue/action

**Diagnostic question:** Is the reader being lectured, or are characters discovering information through conflict?

**Method:** Apply the "Tactical Relevance Test" — does the protagonist need this information to solve their current problem, survive a threat, or manipulate an opponent? If YES, embed in dialogue conflict or active investigation. If NO, cut entirely. Use the observer POV to naturally surface exposition through questions (the "Cicero explained to me" technique).

See `references/exposition-extraction.md` for the diagnostic protocol and surgical fixes.

### Pass 3: Pacing Surgery

**Goal:** Cut research-flexing and maintain thriller velocity

**Diagnostic question:** Am I showing off my research, or serving the story?

**Method:** Paragraph-level audit. Delete research paragraphs (architectural history, meal ingredients, genealogies), keep ONE sensory anchor per scene. Compress dead zones into single transitional sentences. The Hemingway Iceberg: research deeply, show sparingly.

See `references/pacing-surgery.md` for quantitative benchmarks (word counts, dialogue ratios) and cutting techniques.

### Pass 4: Structural Integrity Check

**Goal:** Verify thriller beats survive the historical framework

**Diagnostic question:** Do the thriller mechanics still work, or has historical chronology hijacked the structure?

**Method:** Overlay manuscript with thriller architecture — hook (immediate threat, not world-building), midpoint (reactive → proactive shift), escalating stakes (macro-political → micro-personal), and ending (resolves protagonist's arc, not just historical outcome). Harris's focus: "Men in enclosed rooms going crazy."

See `references/structural-integrity.md` for the full thriller beat checklist.

## Workflow Summary

1. **Pass 1** - Modern analog filter (1-2 days)
2. **Pass 2** - Exposition extraction (2-3 days)
3. **Pass 3** - Pacing surgery (3-4 days, most intensive)
4. **Pass 4** - Structural integrity check (1 day)

Total: ~7-10 days for a full manuscript revision focused on thriller elements.

## Common Over-Corrections

**White Room Syndrome:** Cutting so much description that characters exist in a featureless void

- Fix: Retain sensory anchors (one vivid detail per scene)

**Over-compression:** Stripping all slower moments, losing character development

- Fix: Thrillers need dynamic pacing - moments of quiet dread before action

**Over-modernization:** Making 16th century courtiers sound like corporate executives

- Fix: Balance modern urgency with period vocabulary (save this for authenticity pass)

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

1. **Documentary Anti-Pattern:** Sacrificing thriller pacing for minor historical accuracy
   - Harris: Books succeed by being "extremely accurate and completely fantastical"
   - Choose readability over pedantic accuracy

2. **Passive Observer Anti-Pattern:** Protagonist merely witnesses events
   - Fix: Give them urgent goals that create conflict with the setting

3. **Omniscient Historian Anti-Pattern:** Narrative explains long-term significance
   - Fix: Characters must operate with limited information, no hindsight
