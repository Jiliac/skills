# Boundary: Life Direction ↔ Self-Exploration ↔ Performance Coaching

## The Three-Phase Model

| Phase | Skill              | Core Question           | Input                           | Output                              |
| ----- | ------------------ | ----------------------- | ------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| 1     | Self-Exploration   | Who am I?               | Life experience, patterns       | Personal operating manual           |
| 2     | Life Direction     | What do I build?        | The operating manual            | Direction Architecture Document     |
| 3     | Performance Coach  | How do I execute?       | The direction document          | Accountability, action, results     |

## When to Redirect to Self-Exploration (Phase 1)

Direction-setting surfaces identity material. Redirect when:

- **Schema flooding:** The compensatory goal filter triggers intense emotional responses (distress > 7/10) that the user can't regulate in-session
- **Identity confusion:** "I don't know who I am" rather than "I don't know what to do." The means inventory reveals blank spaces where self-knowledge should be.
- **Trauma activation:** The father wound or other childhood patterns dominate the conversation. Direction becomes about "proving" or "escaping" rather than "building."
- **Parts conflict:** IFS protector parts are actively fighting the direction-setting process — the Analyzer wants more research, the Hider wants a safe option, the Achiever wants the most impressive option.

**How to make the handoff:** "What's coming up right now isn't a direction question — it's a 'who am I' question. The self-exploration skill is better equipped for this. Want to spend a session there before we continue?"

**Coming back:** When the user has processed the material and can distinguish between "I'm unsure about this direction" (legitimate → stay here) and "this direction activates my schema" (emotional → self-exploration first), resume direction-setting.

## When to Redirect to Performance Coaching (Phase 3)

The handoff happens at Session 8 — but sometimes earlier or later.

**Ready for handoff when:**

- Direction Architecture Document is complete (all five sections)
- The user can state their direction in one sentence without hedging
- The somatic commitment ceremony felt genuine (body leaned in, not contracted)
- The first week of the 90-day sprint has begun
- Barriers are about implementation ("how do I find clients?"), not direction ("should I be doing this?")

**Not ready for handoff when:**

- The user keeps revising the direction statement
- The commitment ceremony felt forced or hollow
- Barriers are about direction, not implementation
- The somatic check-in consistently shows contraction or dread (not just discomfort — dread)

**How to make the handoff:** "You have your direction. You've tested it, committed to it, and started executing. The question now is no longer 'what' but 'how.' Performance coaching will hold you accountable and help you push through avoidance. Switch to that skill for your daily and weekly sessions."

**What the performance coach receives:**

- Direction statement (one sentence)
- 12-month target with quarterly milestones
- First 90-day sprint plan with implementation intentions
- Schema alarm system with response protocols
- Revision protocol and calendar

**What the performance coach does NOT do:**

- Question the direction itself (that's for quarterly review)
- Re-run values clarification or schema work (that's self-exploration)
- Generate new direction options (that's this skill)

## The Quarterly Direction Review

Every 3 months, the user returns briefly to this skill (not performance coaching) for a structured check:

1. Re-administer Self-Concordance assessment on current direction
2. Somatic check: "Does this still feel right in my body?"
3. Bull's Eye reassessment: Am I living closer to my values than 3 months ago?
4. Review schema alarm log: Was compensatory motivation taking over?
5. Decision: recommit, revise, or pivot

**Recommit** if: core values still aligned, progress is real even if slow, discomfort is growth-edge not misalignment.

**Revise** if: specific elements need adjustment but overall direction holds. (e.g., change target market but keep the consulting direction)

**Pivot** if: Self-concordance index has dropped significantly, the somatic check consistently shows dread, or the direction fails its own success criteria for two consecutive quarters.

## The "Both" Scenario

Sometimes the user needs direction-setting AND coaching in the same week:

- **Direction session:** When the quarterly review surfaces genuine questions about fit or alignment
- **Coaching sessions:** The rest of the week, executing on the current direction

The key rule: **within a single session, never mix direction-questioning with execution-pushing.** They require opposite stances. Direction-setting is exploratory and open. Coaching is directive and closed. Mixing them produces neither.
