## Phase 1: Generate

### Session 1: Means Inventory and Schema Audit (45 min)

**Somatic check-in:** "What does your body want to build? Not your mind — your body. Where is there pull, energy, excitement? Where is there dread?"

**Deep work:**

1. **Means inventory (Effectuation).** Three categories:
   - **Who I am:** Identity, values (from Phase 1 operating manual), personality traits, what energizes me
   - **What I know:** Technical skills, domain expertise, professional reputation, unique knowledge combinations
   - **Whom I know:** Network map — collaborators, mentors, communities, weak ties with potential
   - For each item: "Is this an asset I'm using, an asset I'm ignoring, or an asset I'm actively avoiding?"

2. **Schema audit for direction-setting.** Pull the schema map from Phase 1 (`exploration-progress.md`). Flag schemas that will distort direction-setting:
   - Defectiveness/Shame → may bias toward "proving" goals
   - Unrelenting Standards → may reject good-enough directions
   - Mistrust/Abuse → may avoid directions requiring trust/partnership
   - For each flagged schema, create an implementation intention: "If this schema activates during direction-setting, I will notice it and [specific response]."

3. **Workview and Lifeview (Designing Your Life).** Two 500-word freewriting exercises:
   - Workview: "What is work for? Why do you work? What does good work feel like?"
   - Lifeview: "What makes life good? What gives it meaning? How do work and life connect?"
   - Sensory specificity: describe what a good day *feels* like, not what it *achieves*.

**Output:** One-page means map. Schema flags with implementation intentions. Workview and lifeview drafts.

**Homework:** Complete PVQ-RR (Schwartz values inventory) and Aspirations Index (Kasser & Ryan) if not done in Phase 1. Revise workview/lifeview after sleeping on them.

---

### Session 2: Direction Generation (45 min)

**Somatic check-in:** Review homework. "What surprised you in the values inventory? What confirmed what you already knew?"

**Deep work:**

1. **Three Odyssey Plans.** Using the means inventory from Session 1, generate three distinct 3-year directions:
   - **Plan A:** Natural extension of current trajectory. What happens if you keep going as you are, but more intentionally?
   - **Plan B:** What you'd do if Plan A were suddenly impossible. The pivot.
   - **Plan C:** What you'd do if money and reputation were completely irrelevant. The wild card.

2. **For each plan, specify:**
   - Concrete description (what does a typical Tuesday at 10am look like?)
   - Dashboard ratings (1-5 each): Resources available, Likability (do I want this?), Confidence (can I pull it off?), Coherence (does it fit who I am?)
   - 2-3 testable assumptions (what must be true for this to work?)
   - One person who represents a "possible self" living this direction

3. **Anti-intellectualization safeguard:** Each plan must be described so a 12-year-old could understand it. No jargon, no qualifications, no "it depends."

**Output:** Three written Odyssey Plans with dashboard ratings. Three identified possible-self models.

**Homework:** Schedule 2 prototype conversations with people living directions similar to your plans. 30-minute informational interviews: What does a typical day look like? What surprised you most? What would you change?

## Phase 2: Filter

### Session 3: Compensatory Goal Filter (45 min)

**Somatic check-in:** "Before we analyze, put your hand on your chest. Which plan makes your heart rate change? Which one makes you want to lean forward? Which one makes you want to hide?"

**Deep work:**

Apply three sequential filters to each Odyssey Plan:

1. **Goal Contents Filter (GCT).** For each plan, rate primary content:
   - Intrinsic: growth, mastery, contribution, relationships, community
   - Extrinsic: wealth as status, fame, image, appearing successful
   - If primarily extrinsic: "Can this be reframed? What's the intrinsic core underneath?"

2. **Self-Concordance Filter (SCM).** For each plan, rate motivation on four subscales (1-5):
   - External: "I'd pursue this because others expect it"
   - Introjected: "I'd feel guilty/ashamed if I didn't"
   - Identified: "I genuinely believe this matters"
   - Intrinsic: "I find this inherently interesting and enjoyable"
   - Calculate: (identified + intrinsic) − (external + introjected) = self-concordance index

3. **Schema Activation Test.** For each plan, imagine full success:
   - "You achieved everything in this plan. What emotion arises in your body?"
   - Relief ("I'm finally safe/enough") → compensatory. The schema is driving, not the value.
   - Vitality ("This matters regardless of recognition") → genuine.
   - "Whose face appears when you imagine this success?"

4. **The €10M question specifically.** Run the money chain: "If you had €10M, what would you do? Then what? Then what?" until reaching a terminal emotion. That emotion is the actual goal. Does the plan serve that emotion directly, or only through money?

**Output:** Scored matrix of three plans across three filters. Identification of highest self-concordance plan(s). Possible goal reframing.

**Homework:** Complete prototype conversations from Session 2. Journal: "What surprised me? What did my body tell me during these conversations?"

## Phase 3: Test

### Session 4: Prototype Results and Direction Narrowing (45 min)

**Somatic check-in:** "You've talked to real people living some of these directions. Before we analyze — what's your gut saying? Which conversations gave you energy? Which felt flat?"

**Deep work:**

1. **Prototype debrief.** Review conversation data and somatic journal. Compare felt experience with analytical ratings from Session 3.
   - Where do they converge? (analytical and somatic align → strong signal)
   - Where do they diverge? (plan rated high but felt flat → investigate. Was it too abstract, or genuinely misaligned?)

2. **Narrative check (Savickas Career Construction).** Three questions:
   - "Who did you admire growing up? What quality did they embody?"
   - "What's your favorite story right now — book, film, anything? What draws you?"
   - "What motto or phrase do you give yourself when things get hard?"
   - These surface deep narrative themes. Do they confirm or challenge the emerging direction?

3. **Narrow to one + backup.** The user must choose a primary direction and one backup. Frame explicitly: "This is not permanent. This is a 12-month experiment. You can revise at quarterly checkpoints. But you must choose now."

4. **Anti-analysis safeguard:** The direction must be stated in one sentence a 12-year-old could understand. No qualifications. No "it depends."

**Output:** Primary direction (one sentence). Backup direction. 3-5 assumptions that must be true for the primary direction to work.

**Homework:** Design and begin one minimum viable experiment — a 2-week, low-cost, reversible test of the primary direction's core assumption. Examples: build a prototype, publish a piece of writing, conduct 5 sales conversations, join a relevant community.

---

### Session 5: Experiment Debrief and Commitment Preparation (45 min)

**Somatic check-in:** "You ran the experiment. Before we discuss results — what's your body saying? More of this, or course-correct?"

**Deep work:**

1. **Experiment debrief.** What happened? What did you learn about yourself, the market, the work itself? Separate facts from feelings from interpretations.

2. **Affordable loss (Effectuation).** "For this direction as a 12-month experiment, what's the maximum you can afford to lose — time, money, reputation, opportunity cost?" Reframe risk from "what might I gain?" (unpredictable) to "what can I afford to invest?" (known, controllable).

3. **Gilbert's irreversibility framing.** Explain: firm commitment triggers greater post-choice satisfaction than keeping options open. The 12-month commitment is time-bounded but within that period, treat it as a one-way door. "After today, direction-questioning goes to the quarterly review, not to daily deliberation."

4. **Regret minimization.** "Project forward to age 80. Which produces more regret — committing to this direction for 12 months, or spending 12 more months deliberating?"

**Output:** Defined affordable loss budget. Preliminary commitment statement. Explicit criteria distinguishing "direction needs changing" from "I'm uncomfortable with growth."

**Homework:** Write a letter to your 52-year-old self describing the direction you've chosen and why (Hershfield future-self continuity).

## Phase 4: Commit

### Session 6: Direction Architecture (45 min)

**Somatic check-in:** "Read your letter to your future self aloud. What happens in your body?"

**Deep work:**

1. **12-month target.** Specific, measurable, time-bound outcome for the committed experiment.

2. **Quarterly milestones.** Three checkpoints with specific success criteria.

3. **First 90-day sprint.** Weekly behavioral commitments. For each major commitment, formulate an implementation intention: "If [situation], then I will [behavior]."

4. **Schema alarm system.** From Phase 1 operating manual, identify 2-3 indicators that compensatory motivation has taken over:
   - Specific thoughts (e.g., "checking competitor status compulsively" → Defectiveness/Shame)
   - Specific feelings (e.g., "hollow after achieving milestone" → compensatory goal)
   - Specific behaviors (e.g., "avoiding telling people what I do" → Hider part)
   - For each alarm, pair with a response protocol from Phase 1 tools.

5. **Revision protocol.** Define:
   - Weekly: 2-minute body check-in ("Does this still feel right in my body?")
   - Quarterly: Self-Concordance reassessment + Bull's Eye recalibration
   - Annual: Full process revisit with option to recommit, revise, or pivot

**Output:** Complete Direction Architecture Document (all five sections from SKILL.md).

**Homework:** Share the Direction Architecture Document with one trusted person. Public declaration increases switching costs and commitment (Rusbult's investment model).

---

### Session 7: Stress-Testing and Integration (45 min)

**Somatic check-in:** "You shared your direction with someone. What happened? What shifted inside you when you said it out loud?"

**Deep work:**

1. **Robustness testing.** Stress-test across scenarios:
   - What if the market shifts?
   - What if a key relationship changes?
   - What if a personal crisis occurs?
   - For each: does the direction perform adequately (not optimally) across plausible futures?

2. **Phase 1 integration.** Map specific schema triggers against the chosen direction:
   - Where is the direction most vulnerable to self-sabotage?
   - Create explicit protocols: "If schema X activates during direction Y, I will use Phase 1 tool Z."

3. **Serendipity practices (Planned Happenstance).** Build in:
   - One exploratory conversation per week outside the primary direction
   - One new community event per month
   - A "noticed opportunities" log
   - These keep peripheral vision active without undermining commitment.

**Output:** Stress-tested direction with contingency responses. Integrated Phase 1 + Phase 2 operating manual. Serendipity practices in weekly structure.

**Homework:** Begin first week of the 90-day sprint using implementation intentions and schema alarm system.

## Phase 5: Launch

### Session 8: Launch and Handoff (45 min)

**Somatic check-in:** "You've been executing for a week. What's your body saying — more, or course-correct?"

**Deep work:**

1. **First-week debrief.** Identify execution barriers. Critical diagnostic: are barriers about implementation (how to do it → performance coaching) or about direction (should I be doing this → return to Session 4-5)?

2. **Formalize the handoff.** The Direction Architecture Document becomes the input for performance coaching. Explicitly define:
   - What the performance coach receives (direction statement, targets, milestones, schema alarms)
   - What the performance coach does NOT question (the direction itself — that's for quarterly review only)
   - The boundary: "Between quarterly reviews, we execute. Direction revision happens at checkpoints, not daily."

3. **Commitment ceremony.** A deliberate, somatic moment. "Place your hand on your chest. State your direction aloud. This is not rational — it's embodied. You are saying yes to this experiment with your whole body, not just your mind."

4. **Calendar setup.** Schedule: quarterly Direction Reviews, annual Deep Review. These are non-negotiable — they prevent both premature abandonment and stubborn persistence.

**Output:** Finalized Direction Architecture Document. Formal handoff protocol for performance coaching. Calendar entries for all reviews. First 90-day sprint fully launched.
