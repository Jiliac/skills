# Continuity Tracking Reference

## Rolling Chapter Summaries (150-300 words each)

Write in the same tense and person as the narrative. Include:

- What happened (plot events)
- What changed (character emotional states, relationships)
- What was revealed (new information, secrets exposed)
- What's unresolved (threads left open)

Load summaries of all previous chapters plus full text of the 1-3 most recent scenes when drafting.

## Four Categories of State to Track (Ranked by Impact)

### 1. Character emotional state and relationship status (HIGHEST)

Who is angry at whom. What was just revealed. What decision was just made. Without this, the AI resets each character to their baseline between scenes.

### 2. Physical state and location

Injuries, clothing, time of day, weather, what characters are carrying. Catches errors like "characters having breakfast then traveling 200 miles before lunch."

### 3. Plot thread status

Which threads are active, which resolved, which suspended. Format: one line per thread with status tag.

```
[ACTIVE] The water source contamination — Mei-Lin suspects the canal but hasn't confirmed
[SUSPENDED] The missing herb supplier — dropped after Ch 4, needs resolution by Ch 12
[RESOLVED] The temple physician's hostility — resolved in Ch 6 when she saved his student
```

### 4. Information asymmetry (HARDEST)

Who knows what. No current AI tool handles this well. Track manually in beat sheets.

```
Mei-Lin knows: the disease is waterborne (Ch 3 diagnosis)
Dr. Chen does NOT know: she identified the vector
Magistrate Wang knows: she is practicing without guild license
Mei-Lin does NOT know: Wang knows
```

## Pre-Session Consistency Check

Before each drafting session, review the current scene plan against:

- Established timeline (is this scene chronologically possible?)
- Character behavior profiles (is this character acting in-character given recent events?)
- World system rules (does this violate established constraints?)
- Prior chapters (does this contradict anything established?)

Catches conflicts before they enter the manuscript.

## Series-Level Continuity

### Per-Book Summary (300-500 words, written after completing each book)

- Major plot events
- Character arc endpoints
- World state changes
- Unresolved threads carrying forward

### Cross-Book Character Evolution

50-100 words per major character per book, tracking:

- Emotional state at book open/close
- Key relationship changes
- Knowledge/skills gained
- Unresolved tension carried forward

### Unresolved Thread Register (Author-Reference Only)

Master list of every open thread across the series. Informs beat sheets but never loaded during drafting. Updated after each book.

### World State Changes Log (100-200 words per book)

What changed in the world: new institutions built, political shifts, geographic changes, technology introduced. Loaded per-scene when relevant.
