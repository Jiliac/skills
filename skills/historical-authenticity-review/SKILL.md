# Historical Authenticity Review

Make the manuscript accurate as historical fiction while preserving the thriller urgency established during previous revision. This skill implements a two-pass review focused on dialogue authenticity and comprehensive anachronism detection.

## When to Use

Use this skill when you need to:

- Review dialogue for period authenticity while maintaining thriller tension
- Execute a final anachronism sweep across factual, tonal, and conceptual dimensions
- Balance modern urgency (from thriller revision) with historical believability
- Ensure the manuscript works as HISTORICAL fiction after thriller editing

**Critical:** This skill is designed for use AFTER `thriller-revision`. It resolves the tension between modern urgency and period accuracy by applying them sequentially, not simultaneously.

## What This Skill Does NOT Do

This is focused revision, not drafting or structural editing:

- NOT initial drafting (see `historical-scene-drafting`)
- NOT thriller pacing surgery (see `thriller-revision`)
- NOT research or source evaluation (see `historical-fiction-research`)
- NOT plot restructuring or character development

## The Two Mental Modes

**Skill 4 (`thriller-revision`):** "Make it exciting" - Modernize for urgency
**Skill 5 (This skill):** "Make it accurate" - Ensure period believability

These modes conflict. Modern analog filter wants modern urgency; dialogue authenticity wants period accuracy. By separating them into sequential passes, you navigate this tension deliberately rather than trying to reconcile it during a single revision.

## Core Principles

### 1. Dialogue Must Be Authentic AND Urgent

The primary challenge: Period-appropriate dialogue that maintains thriller velocity.

**The Harris Balance:**

- Natural speech rhythms (not stilted museum prose)
- Period-appropriate vocabulary (not modern slang)
- Thriller tension (not academic formality)

After modern analog filter (Skill 4) has injected urgency, this pass ensures the dialogue hasn't drifted too modern or become emotionally anachronistic.

### 2. Three Types of Anachronism

**Factual** - Objects, technology, events out of place (stirrups in 200 BC)
**Tonal** - Museum prose, overly modern slang, wrong register
**Conceptual** - Modern psychology, political concepts, social norms imposed on historical minds

Conceptual anachronisms are the most insidious and the hardest to detect. See `references/anachronism-final-pass.md` for diagnostic protocols.

### 3. Authenticity Serves the Story

When choosing between pedantic accuracy and narrative drive, choose narrative drive. Harris's books are "both extremely accurate and completely fantastical at the same time." Minor historical divergences are acceptable if they preserve thriller pacing.

The authenticity review catches major breaks in immersion, not every possible deviation from documentary truth.

## Two-Pass Revision Protocol

### Pass 1: Dialogue Authenticity Review

**Mental mode:** "Does this sound like a person from this era under pressure?"

**Diagnostic questions:**

- Does any character speak in long, uninterrupted exposition paragraphs?
- Are characters using modern idioms, slang, or corporate jargon?
- Is the syntax overly archaic ("Prithee," "Forsooth") or stilted?
- Does the dialogue create thriller tension or feel like a lecture?
- Would this line sound natural if read aloud?

**Surgical fixes:**

- Break long speeches with action beats or interruptions
- Replace modern phrases with period-neutral equivalents
- Simplify archaic constructions while maintaining slight "otherness"
- Ensure characters argue, deflect, and manipulate (not just explain)
- Read dialogue aloud as diagnostic tool

See `references/dialogue-review.md` for line-by-line audit protocol.

### Pass 2: Anachronism Sweep (Three Sub-Passes)

**Sub-pass 2a: Factual Anachronisms**

Audit physical objects, technology, geographical features, documented events.

**Questions:**

- Are all material objects period-appropriate? (clothing, weapons, food, architecture)
- Are place names and geographical features accurate for this era?
- Are documented historical events correctly dated and sequenced?

**Fix:** Replace incorrect items with period-accurate equivalents using research from `historical-fiction-research` skill.

**Sub-pass 2b: Tonal Anachronisms**

Audit language, idioms, metaphors, narrative voice.

**Questions:**

- Are idioms or metaphors used before they entered the language?
- Does the narrative voice adopt "museum curator" objectivity?
- Is profanity period-appropriate and class-appropriate?
- Are metaphors drawn from period-specific experiences? (No "clockwork precision" before mechanical clocks)

**Fix:** Replace anachronistic phrases with period-neutral equivalents or cut entirely.

**Sub-pass 2c: Conceptual Anachronisms**

Audit for modern worldviews, psychology, political concepts, social categories imposed on historical characters.

**Questions:**

- Are characters processing emotions using modern therapeutic language?
- Do characters possess modern democratic ideals, feminist frameworks, or progressive values without contextual grounding?
- Are modern political categories (left/right, liberal/conservative) imposed on different historical systems?
- Do characters recognize their era as "primitive" or see themselves with hindsight?

**Fix:** Reframe character thoughts to reflect period-specific belief systems. Characters must operate with limited information and period-specific assumptions. See `references/anachronism-final-pass.md` for detailed diagnostic protocol.

## The Harris Anti-Patterns Checklist

Professional historical thriller writers avoid these failure modes:

1. **The "Documentary" Anti-Pattern** - Prioritizing pedantic accuracy over narrative drive
2. **The "Passive Observer" Anti-Pattern** - Characters as cameras, not active participants
3. **The "Omniscient Historian" Anti-Pattern** - Characters understanding long-term historical significance with hindsight

After completing the two-pass review, audit the manuscript against these anti-patterns. If you've overcorrected for historical accuracy at the expense of thriller urgency, dial back the formality.

See `references/harris-anti-patterns.md` for detailed avoidance strategies.

## Common Over-Corrections

**Warning:** Aggressive authenticity editing can strip away the urgency established in Skill 4.

**White Room Syndrome** - Cutting all description to preserve pace, losing period texture

- Fix: Retain sensory anchors (smell, temperature, material objects)

**Over-Formalization** - Making dialogue so period-appropriate it becomes stilted

- Fix: Maintain natural speech rhythms, use the "sideways shift" technique (Hilary Mantel)

**Conceptual Over-Correction** - Making characters too "of their time," losing emotional accessibility

- Fix: Universal human emotions (fear, ambition, love) transcend eras; only the expression changes

**Pass 2c:** Conceptual anachronism sweep - Worldviews, psychology, social categories

**After this skill:** Manuscript works as both a thriller AND historically believable fiction.
