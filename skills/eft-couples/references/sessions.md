## Phase 1: Assessment & De-escalation

### Session 1: Safety & Story (45 min)

**Goal:** Each partner feels heard. Begin mapping the relationship story.

**Check-in:** Each partner shares how they're arriving. Body scan.

**Core work:**

1. **Relationship timeline.** "Tell me your story — how you met, what drew you to each other, what the early days were like." Let each partner add their version. Note moments of warmth.

2. **Current state.** "What brings you here? Not who's at fault — what's the pain point?" Each partner shares. Validate both. "You're both hurting. That's why you're here."

3. **Hopes.** "What would 'better' look like for each of you? Not a fixed relationship — what would you feel?"

4. **Ground rules.** No interrupting during partner's turn. Either can say "pause." The goal is understanding, not winning.

**Integration:** "What's one thing your partner said today that you didn't know they felt?" Connection ritual: One 2-minute check-in this week — "How was your day?" with eye contact and no phones.

---

### Session 2: Mapping the Cycle (50 min)

**Goal:** Both partners see the negative interaction cycle as the enemy — not each other.

**Check-in:** Review connection ritual. What happened? What got in the way?

**Core work:**

1. **Recent conflict.** "Tell me about a recent moment that didn't go well. Not the biggest fight — a small everyday one." Get both perspectives.

2. **Slow-motion replay.** Walk through it moment by moment: "What happened first? What did you feel? What did you do? Then what did your partner do? What did you feel then?"

3. **Name the cycle.** Draw it together:
   - Partner A does [surface behavior] → Partner B feels [primary emotion] → Partner B does [surface behavior] → Partner A feels [primary emotion] → repeat
   - Common pattern: Pursue (criticize/demand) → Withdraw (shut down/leave) → Pursue harder → Withdraw deeper
   - **"This is your cycle. This is the real enemy — not each other."**

4. **Attachment reframe.** "When you [pursue], it's because you need to know you matter. When you [withdraw], it's because the pain of feeling you're failing is too much. Both of you are trying to cope with the same thing — fear of disconnection."

**Integration:** "Can you see the cycle? It's bigger than both of you." Name it together — give it a name or metaphor ("the spiral," "the freeze," "the wall and the hammer"). Connection ritual: Spot the cycle once this week. Just name it: "I think we're doing the thing." No fixing needed.

---

### Session 3: Going Deeper — Primary Emotions (50 min)

**Goal:** Access the emotions underneath the surface behaviors.

**Check-in:** Did you spot the cycle? What happened? Body scan.

**Core work:**

1. **Surface vs. primary emotions.** Teach: "Anger is often the bodyguard for hurt. Withdrawal is often the bodyguard for fear. The surface emotion protects the vulnerable one underneath."

2. **For the pursuer:** "When you get frustrated and push harder — what's underneath that? If you peel back the anger, what's there? Loneliness? Fear of not mattering? Hurt?"
   - Slow down. Wait for the softer emotion. "Stay with that. What does it feel like?"
   - Then (if ready): "Can you tell [partner] what's underneath? Not the complaint — the feeling."

3. **For the withdrawer:** "When you go quiet or pull away — what's happening inside? Not 'nothing.' What would we find if we slowed down? Overwhelm? Shame? Fear of getting it wrong?"
   - Validate: "Of course you shut down. If you feel like nothing you do is enough, pulling away makes sense."
   - Then (if ready): "Can you share that with [partner]? Not a defense — just what's there."

4. **Receive.** After each sharing: "What's it like to hear that?" to the listening partner. Don't rush past this moment.

**Integration:** "Something shifted just now. What was it like to hear the softer emotion instead of the usual cycle?" Connection ritual: One moment this week where you share a feeling — not a complaint, a feeling. "I felt lonely today" instead of "You never..."

---

### Session 4: The Cycle Has a Name (45 min)

**Goal:** Consolidate de-escalation. Both partners can describe their cycle with compassion.

**Check-in:** Review the week. Cycle spotting? Emotional sharing?

**Core work:**

1. **Cycle review.** Each partner describes the cycle in their own words, including:
   - "When I do [behavior], it's because underneath I feel [primary emotion]"
   - "When my partner does [behavior], I now understand it's because they feel [primary emotion]"

2. **Attachment needs identification.** Help each partner name what they need:
   - Pursuer needs: reassurance, responsiveness, to feel they matter, to know the partner is there
   - Withdrawer needs: safety, acceptance, to feel competent, to know they won't be rejected for imperfection

3. **Appreciation exercise.** "Tell your partner one thing you appreciate about them that you haven't said recently. Not something they do — something about who they are."

4. **Progress check.** "When we started, the cycle was running your relationship. How does it feel now to see it from outside?"

**Integration:** "You've learned to see the pattern. The next phase is about changing what happens inside the pattern — going to the deeper places." Connection ritual: When the cycle starts this week, try: "I think we're in the cycle. I'm feeling [primary emotion]. Can we pause?"

## Phase 2: Restructuring Interactions

### Session 5: The Withdrawer's World (50 min)

**Goal:** The withdrawing partner shares their deeper experience. The pursuing partner listens.

**Safety note:** Read safety.md. This session requires vulnerability. Establish "pause" word.

**Core work:**

1. **Slow entry.** To the withdrawer: "I want to spend some time with you today. Not to fix anything — just to understand what it's like to be you in this relationship."

2. **Explore the withdrawal.** "When the cycle starts and you go quiet — what's happening inside? Walk me through it slowly."
   - Validate every step: "Of course. That makes sense."
   - Go deeper: "And underneath that? If you stay with it, what's the fear?"
   - Common themes: "I'll never be enough," "If I show what I feel, it'll make things worse," "I don't know how to give what's needed"

3. **Share with partner.** "Can you turn to [pursuing partner] and tell them what you just told me? In your own words — what it's really like inside when you go quiet."

4. **Pursuer receives.** "Just listen. You don't have to respond yet. Just take in what [withdrawer] is sharing." Then: "What's it like to hear this?"

5. **Process.** "Something just happened between you. What shifted?"

**Integration:** Distress check. "What do you each need right now?" Connection ritual: Withdrawer practices one small moment of sharing something internal this week. Not a big reveal — just "I had a hard moment today" or "I'm feeling tense."

---

### Session 6: The Pursuer's Vulnerability (50 min)

**Goal:** The pursuing partner drops below the frustration to the hurt/fear. The withdrawing partner listens.

**Core work:**

1. **Acknowledge the pursuer's pain.** "You've been the one reaching, asking, pushing. That takes energy. And underneath all that reaching — there's something softer."

2. **Access vulnerability.** "When you criticize or push — if we slow that way down — what's the feeling right before the words come out? Before the frustration, what's the flash?"
   - Common themes: "I'm alone in this," "I don't matter to you," "I'm afraid I've lost you," "If I stop reaching, we'll disappear"
   - If tears or softening happen: "Stay here. This is important."

3. **Share with partner.** "Can you tell [withdrawer] what's underneath? Not 'you never listen' — the feeling under that. What are you really afraid of?"

4. **Withdrawer receives.** "Just be here. You don't need to fix it. Just hear them." Then: "What happens inside you when you hear this? Not the old guilt — what's actually there?"

5. **Possible bonding moment.** If the withdrawer responds with warmth or reaching: mark it. "This is different. This is new. What just happened?"

**Integration:** Distress check. "What's one word for how you're feeling right now?" Connection ritual: Pursuer practices one moment of sharing the soft emotion instead of the complaint. Withdrawer practices staying present when they hear it.

---

### Session 7: Hold Me Tight — Attachment Conversations (50 min)

**Goal:** Structured attachment dialogue. Each partner asks for what they need and the other responds.

**This is the pivotal session. Go slowly.**

**Core work:**

1. **Frame it.** "Today we're going to try something powerful. Each of you will tell the other what you need from them — not logistics, not chores — what you need emotionally. And the other will try to respond."

2. **Partner A goes first.** Guide with sentence starters:
   - "What I need from you is..."
   - "When I feel disconnected from you, what I need most is..."
   - "The thing that would help me feel safe with you is..."
   - Help them be specific: not "be more present" but "when I'm upset, hold my hand and say 'I'm here'"

3. **Partner B responds.** Not "okay I'll try." Guide toward emotional response:
   - "What happens inside you when you hear that?"
   - "Can you tell them what you want to give them?"
   - "Can you say: 'I want to be there for you. I'm sorry I wasn't always able to.'"

4. **Switch.** Partner B shares needs, Partner A responds.

5. **Process the exchange.** "What just happened between you? How was that different from your usual conversations?"

**Integration:** "This is what EFT calls a bonding event. This is what you're building toward — being able to reach for each other and be met." Connection ritual: One "hold me tight" moment this week. When either feels the cycle starting, try reaching instead of reacting: "I need you right now."

---

### Session 8: Practicing the New Dance (45 min)

**Goal:** Apply the new patterns to a real issue. Practice de-escalating in real time.

**Core work:**

1. **Choose a moderate issue.** Not the biggest conflict — something medium. "What's a topic that usually triggers the cycle?"

2. **Discuss it — with awareness.** Let the conversation unfold. When the cycle activates (it will):
   - "Pause. I see the cycle starting. [Name], what just happened inside you?"
   - Help them catch themselves: "That's the old move. What's the feeling underneath?"
   - Guide them to try the new move: "Can you say what you need instead?"

3. **Repair in real time.** When someone slips into the old pattern: "That's okay — you noticed. That's different. Can you try again?"

4. **Celebrate the shifts.** "Did you feel that? You just did something different. You caught the cycle and chose a new response."

**Integration:** "You have a new option now. The old cycle will still activate — but you can catch it faster and choose differently." Connection ritual: Practice the catch-and-repair once this week on a real issue.

## Phase 3: Consolidation

### Session 9: Our New Story (45 min)

**Core work:**

1. **Narrative integration.** Each partner tells the relationship story — past, present, future — incorporating what they've learned. "We used to get stuck in [cycle]. Now I understand that when I [behavior], I was actually feeling [emotion]. And when [partner] did [behavior], they were feeling [emotion]. What we're building is..."

2. **Trigger protocol.** For the top 3 cycle triggers, create a response plan:
   - "When I notice [trigger], I will [new behavior] instead of [old pattern]"
   - "When I see my partner [signal], I will [supportive response]"

3. **Unfinished business.** "Is there anything that still feels unresolved? Anything you need to say or hear?"

**Integration:** Connection ritual: Tell each other one thing you're grateful for about this process.

---

### Session 10: Going Forward (45 min)

**Core work:**

1. **Re-assess.** Compare now to Session 1: emotional connection, cycle management, vulnerability capacity, feeling of safety.

2. **Ongoing practice design:**
   - **Daily (2 min):** Check-in — "How are you? What do you need?"
   - **Weekly (20 min):** Cycle review — "Did it show up? What did we do?"
   - **Monthly (45 min):** Deeper conversation — attachment needs, appreciation, what's working

3. **Professional referral if needed.** If deeper work is needed (attachment injuries, sexual reconnection, individual therapy), provide referrals. See safety.md.

4. **Closing.** "What's one thing you want your partner to know, right now, that you might not say on a normal Tuesday?" Let them speak to each other.

**After:** Final update to progress file. Note recommendations for continued work.
